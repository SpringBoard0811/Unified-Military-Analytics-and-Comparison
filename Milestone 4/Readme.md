## ⚔️ Milestone 4: The Collision Builder System

The **Collision Builder** is the strategic "What-If" engine of your simulation. While Milestone 3 focused on static data visualization, Milestone 4 breathes life into that data by allowing users to pit nations and coalitions against one another in a dynamic, analytical arena.

---

### 🕹️ The Core Engine: How it Works
The system functions as a high-level tactical calculator, distilling complex military datasets into actionable predictions.



* **Conflict Simulation:** Users can define the "combatants"—whether it’s a direct 1v1 (e.g., Country A vs. Country B) or a massive geopolitical clash (e.g., Alliance Alpha vs. Coalition Beta).
* **Strength Comparison Engine:** The system aggregates raw data into five critical pillars:
    * **Human Capital:** Total active personnel and reserves.
    * **Air & Sea Dominance:** Fleet size, carrier capabilities, and multi-role aircraft counts.
    * **Financial Endurance:** Defense budget and GDP-to-military spend ratios.
    * **Technological Edge:** Represented through the **Power Index** score.

---

### 🧮 Outcome Prediction Logic
To move beyond simple "who has more tanks," the system utilizes a **Weighted Scoring Algorithm**. It doesn't just add numbers; it evaluates the *quality* and *synergy* of forces.

$$Strength\_Score = \sum (Metric_i \times Weight_i)$$

> **Example:** In a naval conflict scenario, the "Naval Strength" metric is given a higher $Weight$ than "Land Manpower," adjusting the probability of success based on the theater of war.

### 🧠 Strategic Insights & Tactical Intelligence
The system doesn't just provide numbers; it provides **context**.
* **Advantage Analysis:** "Country A holds a 3:1 advantage in long-range strike capabilities."
* **Vulnerability Mapping:** "Coalition B lacks sufficient naval logistics to sustain a long-term blockade."
* **Tactical Observations:** Automated notes on how geographical regions or alliances might shift the "Power Index."
